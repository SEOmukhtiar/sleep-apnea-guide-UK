## 📖 Overview

Sleep disorders affect millions of people worldwide. Two commonly confused conditions are **Sleep Apnea** and **Sleep Paralysis**.

Although both occur during sleep, they are entirely different conditions with different causes, symptoms, and treatment approaches.

This repository provides educational information to help readers better understand these sleep disorders.

---

## 🛌 What Is Sleep Apnea?

Sleep apnea is a sleep disorder in which breathing repeatedly stops and starts during sleep. These breathing interruptions reduce oxygen levels and disrupt normal sleep.

### Common Symptoms

- Loud snoring
- Interrupted breathing during sleep
- Gasping or choking while sleeping
- Excessive daytime sleepiness
- Morning headaches
- Poor concentration
- Dry mouth upon waking
- Fatigue throughout the day

---

## 😶 What Is Sleep Paralysis?

Sleep paralysis is a temporary inability to move or speak while falling asleep or waking up. During an episode, the person remains conscious but cannot control voluntary muscles.

Episodes usually last from a few seconds to a couple of minutes.

### Common Symptoms

- Temporary inability to move
- Inability to speak
- Chest pressure
- Fear or anxiety
- Hallucination-like experiences
- Full awareness during paralysis

---

## 📊 Sleep Apnea vs Sleep Paralysis

| Sleep Apnea | Sleep Paralysis |
|-------------|-----------------|
| Repeated breathing interruptions | Temporary muscle paralysis |
| Often accompanied by loud snoring | Occurs during sleep transitions |
| Can reduce oxygen levels | Usually breathing remains normal |
| Causes daytime fatigue | Usually resolves within minutes |
| Requires medical evaluation | Often improves with better sleep habits |

---

## ⚠️ Risk Factors

### Sleep Apnea

- Obesity
- Large neck circumference
- Family history
- Smoking
- Alcohol consumption
- Older age

### Sleep Paralysis

- Sleep deprivation
- Stress and anxiety
- Irregular sleep schedule
- Narcolepsy
- Sleeping on the back

---

## 🩺 Diagnosis

Healthcare professionals may recommend:

- Physical examination
- Medical history review
- Overnight sleep study (Polysomnography)
- Home sleep apnea testing
- Sleep diary

---

## 💊 Treatment

### Sleep Apnea

Treatment options may include:

- CPAP therapy
- Weight loss
- Lifestyle changes
- Oral appliances
- Positional therapy
- Surgery (in selected cases)

### Sleep Paralysis

Management often focuses on:

- Maintaining a regular sleep schedule
- Reducing stress
- Improving sleep hygiene
- Treating underlying sleep disorders
- Consulting a healthcare provider if episodes become frequent

---

## 🌙 Sleep Health Tips

- Sleep 7–9 hours every night
- Maintain a consistent sleep schedule
- Avoid caffeine before bedtime
- Reduce screen time at night
- Exercise regularly
- Maintain a healthy body weight
- Avoid alcohol before sleep
